Orc Offensive Strategy — Bashing Down the Field
Core concept: You have a massive strength advantage over almost every Snotling player. Use it. Your goal is to create a cage, grind forward methodically, and avoid the fouls + flings their big guys can threaten. You do NOT need to rush — a 2–0 win in turn 16 is as good as turn 4.

🏉 Receiving Setup YOUR KICK-OFF

  • Cage formation: Thrower (#5 Wargug) picks up ball. Surround with #3 Krodgat (Blitzer, Mighty Blow), #11 Kaijin (Troll, Guard), and one Big Un.
  • Blitzers on the flanks: #4 Gruhzot sweeps the side opposite the cage to collapse the flank and threaten their ball carrier on defense.
  • Linemen fill LOS: Put 3 Orc Linemen on the Line of Scrimmage to absorb hits. They are your fodder — that's okay.
  • Keep Goblins back: #10 and #12 stay behind the cage. They are TTM delivery vehicles in emergencies, not blockers.
  • Big Un placement: One Big Un in the cage corner (4-dice block potential with Guard assist), one threatening a flank. Their Unsteady is your risk — don't overextend them.

⚙ Cage Advance Plan EACH TURN

  • Step 1 — Clear your path: Use Blitzers to knock down anyone threatening the next cage square. Mighty Blow Blitzer (#3) is your opener on anything that matters.
  • Step 2 — Guard assist: Kaijin's Guard negates their 2-die defensive blocks. Always position him on the most contested square before blocking.
  • Step 3 — Move the cage: Advance 1–2 squares per turn. Don't rush. A 7–8 turn drive is fine.
  • Step 4 — Watch Pump Wagons: If a Pump Wagon (Juggernaut, Stand Firm) is threatening your cage path, dedicate 2 players to it or re-route. Don't let it split your cage.
  • Scoring window: When you're 3–4 squares from their end zone with 2+ turns left, release your Blitzer to score. Don't dive early.

🚨 Pump Wagon Counter CRITICAL

Pump Wagons have Juggernaut (turns Both Down into Push on a blitz) and Stand Firm (can't be pushed). They will try to drive through your cage like a truck. Two countermeasures:

  • Block them, don't blitz: Since Juggernaut only triggers on a Blitz action, use a regular Block with 2 dice. They have no Block skill — you'll get your dice result. You want Knocked Down or Stumble.
  • Surround them early: If you can surround a Pump Wagon with 2+ players at the start of your turn, it can't move next turn on its go. A Pump Wagon in a tight cage of Orc bodies is neutralized.
  • Krodgat's Mighty Blow: When you do knock them down, Mighty Blow on armour breaks. That's how you kill this matchup's biggest threats permanently.

🎯 TTM Option EMERGENCY SCORE

Your Troll Kaijin has Throw Team-Mate and two Goblins. If you're behind late game or the field is clogged, this is a legit scoring option:

  • Position Kaijin 7–8 squares from end zone. Goblin picks up ball or receives a hand-off.
  • Kaijin throws on a 4+ (Loner means you can't re-roll it via team re-rolls — he uses his own). Goblin lands on a 4+ (Right Stuff, Stunty make the catch harder but the throw is fine).
  • Against Snotlings, the field should be clear enough late game that a Goblin can dodge away after landing. Stunty helps dodge past their small bodies.
Use TTM as a surprise — don't telegraph it by positioning early. Snotlings can't really stop it once set up.
Orc Defensive Strategy — Stopping the Snotling Swarm
Key defensive reality: Snotlings WANT to foul you. Their whole game is using numerical superiority (they field more bodies than you) to surround, prone, and foul your stars off the pitch. Do NOT let them get 2+ snotlings adjacent to any of your key players while those players are prone.

🛡 Kick-Off Setup WHEN KICKING

  • Wide zone pressure: Position both Blitzers in the wide zones (columns 1–2 and 12–13). Snotlings want to spread wide and swarm. Contest the flanks immediately.
  • 3 on LOS: Place Orc Linemen on LOS — they're strong enough to push back Snotlings and create traffic. Don't put Big Uns on LOS (Unsteady is a liability here).
  • Deep safety: Keep Kaijin (Guard) one row back from LOS in the middle. He'll provide assists to your LOS fight and is too valuable to lose to a counter-charge.
  • Kick short and central: Force the Pump Wagons to come to you in the middle of the field where your strength advantage concentrates. Don't kick into the wide zones where they can spread.

🎯 Priority Targets KILL ORDER

  1. Pump Wagon #2 (Sneaky Git) — Top kill target. Sneaky Git means it never risks being sent off for fouling. Remove it ASAP.
  2. Stilty Runna #8 (Lethal Flight / Eye Gouge) — She will try to leap into a crowded area and Eye Gouge. Krodgat's Mighty Blow can remove her.
  3. Both Fungus Flingas — Bombardier can scatter Snotlings across the field unpredictably. They're fragile once you get hands on them.
  4. Trained Trolls — Lower priority since Really Stupid makes them unreliable. Surround and ignore if possible.

🚫 Foul Prevention CRITICAL

Snotlings will pile on every prone player. Their Dirty Player Pump Wagons and the numbers to surround are their main path to injuring you. Counter-measures:

  • Get up fast: Never leave a star player prone in the middle of the pack if you have a Stand Up action available. Your MA6 Orc Linemen can afford to waste a turn standing.
  • Protect with adjacency: Keep a standing player adjacent to any prone star. A Snotling can't foul if your player will be in their tackle zone (they'd have to blitz first). Even a Goblin can serve as a "foul shield."
  • Big Uns: Thick Skull saves you: With Thick Skull, your Big Uns are on a 9+ armour break (effectively), even without rolling. They can take fouling better than most. Still protect them — Pump Wagon's Dirty Player is -1 armour.
  • Troll Kaijin: Regeneration safety net: Even if he gets injured, he may come back. Still protect him — losing Guard hurts your whole offence.
  • Blitzers are your most vulnerable stars: AV8 with no Block protection on the ground (Blitzers have Block naturally but are on 8 armour). Keep them upright or protected at all costs.
Snotling Team Threat Analysis

🚨 Pump Wagons — #1 & #2 PRIORITY THREATS

Why they're dangerous: Juggernaut makes them treat Both Down as a Push when blitzing — they'll crash through your cage if you let them Blitz into it. Stand Firm means you can't push them away. Both have Dirty Player, and #2 has Sneaky Git (never ejected for fouling).

Pump Wagon #2 with Sneaky Git is your #1 kill target this game. It will foul your Blitzers and Big Uns all game without consequence. Get Krodgat's Mighty Blow on it turn 1 if possible.

Counter: Use Block actions (not Blitz) to engage them — Juggernaut doesn't apply. 2-die blocks with a Guard assist. On offence, re-route your cage to avoid Stand Firm positions.

⚡ Niehuddwa — Stilty Runna #8 DANGER

This player has Lethal Flight (when thrown as a TTM, she can cause injury on landing) and Eye Gouge (can permanently blind a player — causes -1 AG permanently on a roll).

Eye Gouge is a permanent stat debuff. If she lands adjacent to your Thrower or a Blitzer and gouges, they lose AG permanently. She only needs to be prone in your tackle zone — watch for setup combinations where their Trolls can TTM her into your backfield.

Counter: Mark her with a Lineman whenever possible. If a Trained Troll is within TTM range of her and there's open space near your ball carrier, reposition. She's fragile — AV6 Stunty — knock her down hard.

💣 Fungus Flingas — #5 Hiligbit & #6 Mr. D.E.D. DISRUPTION

Bombardier means they can throw Bomb-style attacks (Scatter, can hit anyone). Secret Weapon means they're ejected on a 1–3 after the half if they threw a bomb. Sidestep means you can't push them where you want.

Counter: They throw scatter bombs — dodge away from clusters when they're in range. Mark them so they can't throw freely. They're AV6 Stunty — one hit from Krodgat should remove one.

🐸 The Snotling Swarm — Numbers Game ATTRITION

Snotlings (and Fun-Hoppas) have Dodge, Sidestep, and Stunty. That means:

  • Dodge: They dodge out of tackle zones on a 3+ (after modifiers). Don't count on trapping them easily.
  • Sidestep: They choose where they go when pushed. Your pushes are useless for controlling them — only knockdowns matter.
  • Stunty: They're hit on -1 die count from their perspective, but they also dodge on the lower target. Net: fragile but slippery.
  • Fun-Hoppas (2 extra, not shown): Have Leap — can jump 2 squares into your cage. Be ready for a Goblin-from-nowhere play.
On defence, you do NOT need to chase every Snotling. Let them have the wide zones — clog the middle with your Orc mass and force them to go through your strength rather than around it.
Match Plan — Pre-Game to Final Whistle

🎯 Win Condition UNDERSTAND THIS

You are the heavy bash favourite. Snotlings are the lowest-tier team in the game for survivability. Your goal is simple: kill or injure the Pump Wagons and Stilty Runna, then grind out 2 touchdowns while keeping your Blitzers alive.

Do NOT try to score fast. Each Orc on the pitch applying pressure is worth more than an early TD that leaves you defending on a full Snotling board.

🏆 Turn-by-Turn Priority List

  1. First action every turn: Assess Pump Wagon positions. If either is threatening your cage or ball carrier, address it before anything else.
  2. Use Krodgat (Mighty Blow Blitzer) to target high-value kills — Pump Wagons first, then Stilty Runna #8. Mighty Blow is your best tool for removing their stars.
  3. Keep Kaijin (Guard Troll) centrally placed. His Guard assist is the reason you can win 2-die blocks against their stronger pieces.
  4. Protect prone stars immediately — get a standing player adjacent to them OR stand them up before the Snotling player phase begins fouling.
  5. Advance the cage methodically. Don't expose the ball carrier. Every turn the cage moves 1–2 squares forward is good.
  6. When you have 3+ players ahead in the end zone corridor and 2–3 turns left, cut the Blitzer loose to score. Don't wait too long.
  7. On defence after your first TD, blitz their ball carrier immediately. Snotlings struggle to protect the ball — you have the strength to rip it away.

✅ Do This

  • Cage every drive — never carry the ball exposed
  • Block Pump Wagons (not Blitz) to deny Juggernaut
  • Stack Guard + Block assists before engaging their big guys
  • Kill Sneaky Git Pump Wagon as priority #1
  • Use TTM as a late surprise if the field is clogged
  • Mark the Stilty Runna — deny Eye Gouge setups
  • Kick short and central to deny wide spread
  • Accept you'll lose LOS players — linemen are fodder

❌ Avoid This

  • Blitzing Pump Wagons — gives them Juggernaut free pushes
  • Leaving prone Blitzers unprotected adjacent to Snotlings
  • Chasing individual Snotlings — waste of actions
  • Spreading your cage too wide — Snotlings will surround
  • Letting Fun-Hoppas leap into your cage unchecked
  • Scoring on turn 3–4 — gives them too many turns with bodies
  • Putting Big Uns on LOS — Unsteady + Dirty Player = injury risk
  • Ignoring the Fungus Flingas — one scatter bomb can scatter your cage

📊 CTV Advantage — Inducements 140k AHEAD

You have 140 CTV over them. They receive 140k in inducements. Watch for:

  • Bribe (100k): Protects a player from ejection when committing a foul. Combined with Sneaky Git Pump Wagon, this could mean unlimited fouling.
  • Extra Team Re-roll (100k): Gives them one more re-roll for the game.
  • Blood Lust Star (varies): Some Snotling stars are cheap — Fungus Flingas or similar nuisance players could be hired.
Most likely they take a Bribe. Assume the Sneaky Git Pump Wagon will never be ejected and plan your whole game around removing it via injury, not hoping for an ejection roll.
